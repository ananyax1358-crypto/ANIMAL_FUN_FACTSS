ELEPHANTS


Elephants show grief, mourning, and death awareness Elephants return to the bones of dead herd members, gently touching skulls and tusks. They show reduced appetite and social withdrawal after a lossâ€”patterns strikingly similar to human grief responses. This suggests emotional memory and possibly a concept of death, not just reaction.


DOLPHINS

Mirror self-recognition is considered a marker of self-awareness. Dolphins inspect marked areas on their bodies when seeing their reflection. Only a few species pass this test: dolphins, elephants, great apes, magpies, and humans. This suggests a sense of self as an object, not just stimulus-response behavior.
